Global Markets

The US stock market is facing headwinds as the April CPI registered at 3.8%, marking the hottest reading since May 2023. This inflation stickiness, driven heavily by crude oil prices, pushed the 30-year T-bond yield above the critical 5% level, weighing down the Nasdaq and S&P 500.

In contrast, the global memory semiconductor sector is experiencing a historic surge. Driven by AI demand and supply shortages, DRAM contract prices are expected to rise 58%-63% in Q2. Inventories at Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron are practically depleted. Compounding the supply crunch, Samsung's wage negotiations collapsed on May 12, introducing the risk of a major strike that could further tighten memory chip availability globally.
